# SOP-014: Printer White Streak Troubleshooting

## Title

Printer White Streak

## Symptoms

- White streaks or missing vertical lines on printed output.
- Pale bands across text, label, or barcode areas.
- Repeated blank marks on consecutive samples.

## Possible Cause

- Dirty encoder strip.
- Ink starvation or low toner.
- Clogged nozzle or printhead channel.
- Contaminated media path.
- Transfer roller wear or uneven pressure.
- Incorrect print density or calibration.

## Inspection

1. Stop the affected printer and identify machine ID, lot number, shift, media type, and consumable ID.
2. Print a nozzle check, test pattern, or density verification sample.
3. Compare the sample against the approved master sample.
4. Inspect the printhead/nozzle, encoder strip, media path, toner path, and transfer roller.
5. Capture a defect photo and attach it to the quality record.

## Countermeasure

1. Clean the printhead/nozzle and media path using approved lint-free material.
2. Run one approved cleaning cycle.
3. Replace ink, toner, or printhead if two cleaning cycles do not restore the missing lines.
4. Replace transfer roller when repeated streak spacing or visible surface damage is found.
5. Print three consecutive OK samples before releasing the printer back to production.
